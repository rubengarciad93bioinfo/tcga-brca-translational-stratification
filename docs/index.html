Question

How do breast cancer molecular subtypes differ at the transcriptomic and genomic level?

Core result

Basal-like tumours were enriched for TP53 mutation, while HER2-enriched tumours showed strong ERBB2 amplification.

Why it matters

The workflow links RNA-seq, mutation and copy-number data into interpretable patient stratification hypotheses.

What was the biological question?

This project does not ask whether a patient has cancer. TCGA-BRCA mainly contains breast cancer samples. The goal is to understand how breast cancer samples differ molecularly, and how transcriptomic subtypes relate to genomic driver events.

Key finding 1 — RNA-seq profiles reflect molecular subtype structure

PCA of highly variable genes shows that breast cancer molecular subtypes are partly encoded in global transcriptomic profiles. Basal-like samples show the clearest separation, while Luminal A, Luminal B and HER2-enriched samples partially overlap.

PCA using highly variable RNA-seq features. The result supports subtype-associated transcriptomic structure, but also shows that simple unsupervised clustering does not perfectly reproduce subtype labels.

Key finding 2 — Subtypes show interpretable expression signatures

Differential expression analysis recovered expected subtype-associated biology: luminal markers in Luminal A/B, proliferative and basal-like features in Basal-like tumours, and ERBB2/HER2-associated signal in HER2-enriched tumours.

Median expression of exploratory subtype signature genes, z-scored by gene across subtypes. The heatmap summarizes subtype-associated expression programs.

Key finding 3 — Genomic drivers differ by subtype

Mutation and copy-number data reveal distinct alteration patterns across molecular subtypes. This is the core multi-omic result of the project.

Selected mutation and copy-number event frequencies by subtype. Basal-like tumours are enriched for TP53 mutation; HER2-enriched tumours show ERBB2 amplification; Luminal A shows higher frequencies of PIK3CA, CDH1 and MAP3K1 mutations.

HER2-enriched

Luminal A/B

Higher PIK3CA, GATA3, CDH1 and MAP3K1 mutation patterns, with LumB showing more proliferative features.

Patient stratification summary

Molecular subtype was used as the first stratification layer. Mutation, copy-number and expression data were then added to describe what makes each tumour group biologically distinct.

Basal-like

Observed profile: high TP53 mutation frequency, with additional MYC amplification and PTEN/RB1 loss signals.

Stratification value: identifies a more genomically altered and proliferative tumour group that may require different biological hypotheses from luminal disease.

HER2-enriched

Observed profile: frequent ERBB2 amplification together with high ERBB2 expression.

Stratification value: links a transcriptomic subtype to a concrete genomic event and expression readout.

Luminal A

Observed profile: higher PIK3CA, CDH1 and MAP3K1 mutation frequencies, with comparatively low TP53 mutation frequency.

Stratification value: represents a hormone/luminal-associated molecular context with distinct driver alteration patterns.

Luminal B

Observed profile: luminal identity with stronger proliferative and copy-number-associated features than Luminal A.

Stratification value: captures an intermediate group between Luminal A-like biology and more aggressive expression programs.

Normal-like

Observed profile: retained as an annotated subtype, but with fewer samples and less distinctive driver alteration patterns.

Stratification value: included for completeness, while avoiding strong conclusions due to sample size and possible tissue-composition effects.

Subtype-adjusted layer

Observed profile: some alteration-expression links remain after adjusting for subtype, while others are mostly subtype-context effects.

Stratification value: helps separate direct genomic dosage effects from broader subtype-associated biology.

Key finding 4 — Copy-number events have expression consequences

Copy-number alterations were linked with RNA expression to test whether genomic events had measurable transcriptomic consequences.

ERBB2-amplified samples show markedly higher ERBB2 expression, validating copy-number/RNA-seq integration.
ERBB2 amplification connects HER2-enriched subtype identity with high ERBB2 expression.
Subtype-adjusted interpretation: ERBB2 amplification, CCND1 amplification and PTEN deletion remained associated with expression changes after accounting for molecular subtype. In contrast, the apparent PIK3CA mutation–ESR1 relationship largely disappeared after subtype adjustment, suggesting a luminal-context effect rather than a direct expression effect.

Exploratory discovery layer

To move beyond known positive controls, subtype-adjusted scans were used to identify dosage-sensitive genes, driver-associated expression programs and residual co-expression modules. These results are hypothesis-generating. This section is included as an advanced exploratory extension rather than as a claim of novel biomarker discovery.

Subtype-adjusted driver-associated expression effects. This analysis asks whether selected driver events are associated with broader expression programs beyond subtype labels.
Associations between driver events and subtype-adjusted expression modules. These modules require further interpretation and external validation.
Important limitation: CNA-expression hits may reflect broad amplified or deleted genomic regions rather than independent gene-specific driver effects. A next refinement would map candidates to genomic coordinates and distinguish focal events from broad amplicons.

Secondary analysis — clinical outcome exploration

Overall survival means time until death or last follow-up. Progression-free survival means time until disease progression, recurrence or last follow-up. These variables were explored as an additional clinical layer, not as the main result of the project.

Result: molecular subtype showed a modest exploratory association with overall survival, while selected individual driver alterations did not show robust standalone associations with overall survival or progression-free survival in this retrospective TCGA cohort.
Kaplan-Meier analysis by molecular subtype. This result is exploratory and should not be interpreted as clinical validation.

Why this is relevant to translational computational biology

The main value of this project is not a single predictive model, but a reproducible workflow for integrating heterogeneous cancer data and turning it into interpretable stratification hypotheses.

Patient stratification

Uses molecular subtype as an initial tumour grouping and refines it with mutation, copy-number and expression evidence.

Multi-omic integration

Harmonizes RNA-seq, mutation, copy-number and clinical metadata at sample level.

Genotype-expression reasoning

Connects genomic alterations such as ERBB2 amplification or PTEN deletion with expression-level consequences.

Disease mechanism hypotheses

Identifies driver-associated expression patterns and subtype-adjusted signals that can guide follow-up analyses.

Clinical caution

Explores survival metadata while clearly separating exploratory associations from clinically validated biomarkers.

Communication

Summarizes technical analyses into decision-oriented findings, limitations and next analytical steps.

Limitations

  • TCGA is retrospective and not a clinical trial.
  • Treatment and response data are incomplete.
  • Molecular subtype labels are partly expression-derived.
  • Copy-number events may span broad genomic regions, so gene-level CNA-expression effects require regional interpretation.
  • Discovery analyses are hypothesis-generating and require independent validation.
