TL;DR

I built a reproducible multi-omic workflow using TCGA-BRCA to explore how breast cancer molecular subtypes relate to RNA-seq expression, mutation patterns, copy-number alterations and clinical metadata.

The strongest results were the subtype-specific driver alteration patterns: Basal-like tumours were enriched for TP53 mutation, HER2-enriched tumours for ERBB2 amplification, and Luminal A tumours for PIK3CA/CDH1/MAP3K1 alterations.

What was the biological question?

This project does not ask whether a patient has cancer. TCGA-BRCA mainly contains breast cancer samples. The goal is to understand how breast cancer samples differ molecularly, and how transcriptomic subtypes relate to genomic driver events.

Key finding 1 — Global expression captures subtype structure

PCA of highly variable genes shows that breast cancer molecular subtypes are partly encoded in global transcriptomic profiles. Basal-like samples show the clearest separation, while Luminal A, Luminal B and HER2-enriched samples partially overlap.

PCA using highly variable RNA-seq features. The result supports subtype-associated transcriptomic structure, but also shows that simple unsupervised clustering does not perfectly reproduce subtype labels.

Key finding 2 — Subtypes show interpretable expression signatures

Differential expression analysis recovered expected subtype-associated biology: luminal markers in Luminal A/B, proliferative and basal-like features in Basal-like tumours, and ERBB2/HER2-associated signal in HER2-enriched tumours.

Median expression of exploratory subtype signature genes, z-scored by gene across subtypes. The heatmap summarizes subtype-associated expression programs.

Key finding 3 — Genomic drivers differ by subtype

Mutation and copy-number data reveal distinct alteration patterns across molecular subtypes. This is the core multi-omic result of the project.

Selected mutation and copy-number event frequencies by subtype. Basal-like tumours are enriched for TP53 mutation; HER2-enriched tumours show ERBB2 amplification; Luminal A shows higher frequencies of PIK3CA, CDH1 and MAP3K1 mutations.

Key finding 4 — Copy-number events have expression consequences

Copy-number alterations were linked with RNA expression to test whether genomic events had measurable transcriptomic consequences.

ERBB2-amplified samples show markedly higher ERBB2 expression, validating copy-number/RNA-seq integration.
ERBB2 amplification connects HER2-enriched subtype identity with high ERBB2 expression.
Subtype-adjusted interpretation: ERBB2 amplification, CCND1 amplification and PTEN deletion remained associated with expression changes after accounting for molecular subtype. In contrast, the apparent PIK3CA mutation–ESR1 relationship largely disappeared after subtype adjustment, suggesting a luminal-context effect rather than a direct expression effect.

Exploratory discovery layer

To move beyond known positive controls, subtype-adjusted scans were used to identify dosage-sensitive genes, driver-associated expression programs and residual co-expression modules. These results are hypothesis-generating.

Subtype-adjusted driver-associated expression effects. This analysis asks whether selected driver events are associated with broader expression programs beyond subtype labels.
Associations between driver events and subtype-adjusted expression modules. These modules require further interpretation and external validation.
Important limitation: CNA-expression hits may reflect broad amplified or deleted genomic regions rather than independent gene-specific driver effects. A next refinement would map candidates to genomic coordinates and distinguish focal events from broad amplicons.

Clinical outcome exploration

Clinical outcome metadata were integrated as an exploratory layer. Overall survival showed a modest association with molecular subtype, while individual driver events did not show robust standalone OS/PFS associations in this retrospective cohort.

Kaplan-Meier analysis by molecular subtype. This result is exploratory and should not be interpreted as clinical validation.

Limitations

  • TCGA is retrospective and not a clinical trial.
  • Treatment and response data are incomplete.
  • Molecular subtype labels are partly expression-derived.
  • Copy-number events may span broad regions, so gene-level CNA-expression effects require regional interpretation.
  • Discovery analyses are hypothesis-generating and require independent validation.
